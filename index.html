LuminaSynodic

lu·mi·na /ˈluːmɪnə/ noun lights, luminous bodies, and the measured flow of visible radiance.
syn·od·ic /sɪˈnɒdɪk/ adjective relating to recurring celestial alignments observed from Earth.
Etymology Notes
lumina traces to Latin roots for light and sight, grounding the app in perception and clarity.
synodic comes from periodic conjunction cycles, tying interpretation to orbital timing.
